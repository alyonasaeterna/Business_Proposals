Section 01

Objective & Category Focus

The goal is to identify product categories that combine strong project value, design appeal, and cost efficiency for long-term use across interior projects.

High usability + repeatability + sourcing advantage
Category Examples Why It Matters
Decorative & Smart Lighting Chandeliers, pendants, wall lights, smart lighting systems High visual impact, broad product variety, and strong price advantage in global clusters.
Smart Home & Automation Smart locks, switches, sensors, curtain motors Helps create premium, modern project offerings with differentiated value.
Bathroom & Kitchen Fittings Sanitary ware, faucets, shower systems, accessories Useful for both compact and premium projects with better material options.
Surface & Finish Materials Decorative panels, laminates, wall finishes, textures Critical for visual differentiation and premium look within controlled budgets.
Modular Hardware Sliding systems, concealed mechanisms, fittings Improves functionality and space efficiency for modern residential interiors.
Décor & Accent Products Mirrors, décor accessories, designer elements Ideal for finishing touches and future small-volume or wholesale opportunities.
Section 02

Target Sourcing Clusters

These locations are selected because they concentrate large numbers of relevant suppliers, making it practical to evaluate multiple product options in a short time.

Cluster-based sourcing, not random supplier hunting
Market Country Key Products Indicative Scale Why This Matters
Foshan China Furniture, panels, sanitary ware, surfaces Large manufacturing cluster Strong one-region sourcing base for core interior materials and product comparisons.
Zhongshan (Guzhen) China Lighting, LED, smart lighting Major lighting hub High product density, design variety, and competitive pricing for lighting categories.
Shenzhen China Automation, smart home, connected devices Global electronics center Best suited for advanced, automated, and higher-tech home products.
Yiwu China Décor, mirrors, small accessories Mass supplier ecosystem Ideal for broad assortment, quick sourcing, and lower-MOQ decorative items.
Vietnam / Malaysia Alternative Furniture and selected interior products Backup sourcing options Useful nearby alternatives for comparison, diversification, and future expansion.

Why the Trip is Valuable

The purpose is not just to visit markets, but to build a sourcing blueprint that improves product options, cost structure, and project competitiveness over time.

  • Evaluate multiple suppliers in concentrated clusters instead of isolated searches
  • Compare quality, price, customization, and practical project fit on-ground
  • Create a reusable supplier base for future projects and repeat procurement
  • Identify premium-looking products that can still fit budget-sensitive projects

What This Exploration Should Answer

  • Which categories create the strongest design and cost advantage
  • Which markets are most practical for ongoing sourcing
  • What landed-cost opportunities exist versus local alternatives
  • What operational structure should be built after exploration

Section 04

How the Work Moves Forward

A phased approach ensures the trip leads to a practical commercial base rather than just general market exposure.

1
Scope Lock
Finalize categories, design priorities, budget range, and decision parameters before travel.
2
Market Exploration
Visit clusters, compare suppliers, collect options, and shortlist viable products.
3
Evaluation
Review fit, pricing, quality, and project relevance; identify practical sourcing routes.
4
Build Next System
Convert findings into a sourcing model, supplier base, and long-term procurement plan.

Supplier Base Initial supplier network and sourcing routes from high-density clusters.
